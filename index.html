What You’re Seeing

This explorer shows how time and light behave near a massive black hole (think Interstellar’s Gargantua). The slider sets your radius r in units of the Schwarzschild radius Rs. The blue planet moves closer or farther from the black hole based on how you move the slider.

Gravitational time dilation

A stationary clock at radius r ticks slower than a distant clock by γ = 1 / sqrt(1 – Rs/r). The Near Clock shows this slower proper time.

Gravitational redshift

Light climbing out of a gravity well loses energy (longer wavelength). The redshift is z = γ – 1. The color chip and planet tint shift toward red as z grows.

Orbital motion (optional)

If you check “Include orbital motion”, we use the circular-orbit time dilation (gravity + velocity). For Schwarzschild spacetime, the total factor is γ = 1 / sqrt(1 – 3Rs/(2r)) (valid for r ≥ 1.5 Rs; stable for r ≥ 3 Rs).
